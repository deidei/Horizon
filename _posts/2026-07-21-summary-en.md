## [EU Court Rules VPNs Are Lawful Tools in Copyright Case](https://www.techradar.com/vpn/vpn-privacy-security/vpns-are-lawful-technical-tools-says-eu-court-in-landmark-anne-frank-copyright-ruling) ⭐️ 8.0/10

The EU Court of Justice ruled that VPNs are lawful technical tools in a landmark copyright case, setting a precedent for their use across EU member states. This ruling is significant because it protects VPNs from being characterized as illegal circumvention tools in copyright disputes, which could have widespread implications for digital rights and privacy across Europe. The case was brought by the Anne Frank Fonds, which argued that VPNs could be used to access copyrighted material blocked in certain countries, but the court rejected this characterization.

hackernews · healsdata · Jul 21, 19:43 · [Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48997221)

**Background**: The Anne Frank Fonds lawsuit sought to hold VPN services liable for enabling access to the Anne Frank diary in countries where copyright protection had not expired. The EU Court of Justice, however, distinguished between the tool itself and its potential misuse, affirming that VPNs serve legitimate privacy and security purposes.

**Discussion**: Comments highlight that the ruling is specifically about copyright, not censorship or surveillance, as noted by one user. Some discuss implications for age verification laws and VPN bans, with concerns that large social media platforms may decline while private communities rise.

## [Apple wins case over iCloud CSAM scanning refusal](https://blog.ericgoldman.org/archives/2026/07/apple-defeats-liability-for-not-scanning-icloud-for-csam-but-the-judge-was-not-pleased-amy-v-apple.htm) ⭐️ 8.0/10

A court ruled that Apple is not liable for failing to scan iCloud for child sexual abuse material (CSAM), rejecting arguments that Apple's privacy-focused approach violated laws. This ruling could set a precedent for tech companies' obligations regarding encryption and content scanning, influencing the ongoing debate between privacy and child safety. The judge expressed displeasure but ruled that Apple's end-to-end encryption and lack of CSAM scanning do not constitute a violation of law, highlighting the tension between legal expectations and technical feasibility.

hackernews · speckx · Jul 21, 14:31 · [Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48992870)

**Background**: Client-side scanning is a controversial technique that would scan content on users' devices before encryption, which privacy advocates argue undermines security. Apple had previously proposed a system called NeuralHash for detecting CSAM on-device but faced backlash and postponed it. This case centers on whether companies must implement such scanning.

## [Poolside.ai Releases Laguna S 2.1, a 118B MoE Model](https://poolside.ai/blog/introducing-laguna-s-2-1) ⭐️ 8.0/10

Poolside.ai released Laguna S 2.1, an open-weights 118B parameter Mixture of Experts (MoE) language model that is competitive with DeepSeek V4 Flash on coding tasks. This marks one of the first US-based open-weight models to rival DeepSeek V4 Flash, potentially accelerating innovation in coding-focused AI and providing a strong alternative for developers. The model has 118B total parameters with only 8B activated per token, making it efficient for inference while maintaining high quality. It is available under open weights, allowing community quantization and fine-tuning.

hackernews · rexledesma · Jul 21, 17:17 · [Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48995261)

**Background**: Mixture of Experts (MoE) is an architecture where different subsets of parameters ('experts') are activated for each input, enabling larger total parameter counts with lower computational cost. DeepSeek V4 Flash is a 284B total parameter MoE model from Chinese AI lab DeepSeek, known for strong coding performance. Laguna S 2.1 aims to match or exceed its capabilities with fewer total parameters.

## [Jellyfin Co-Founders All Resign Within a Week](https://cybernews.com/tech/jellyfin-founders-step-down-future-uncertain/) ⭐️ 8.0/10

All three co-founders of the open-source media server Jellyfin resigned within a week, citing burnout, disagreements over development direction, and personal reasons. The project's future is uncertain as no succession plan has been announced. Jellyfin is one of the most popular free media server alternatives to proprietary solutions like Plex and Emby, and the simultaneous departure of its entire founding team raises questions about project sustainability and governance. This event also highlights the growing problem of burnout among open-source maintainers. Founder Joshua Boniface left due to severe burnout and mental health risks, Andrew Rabert departed over development direction disagreements and negative community feedback, and Anthony Lavado left concurrently for personal life changes. The team had previously complained in May about AI code submissions exacerbating burnout, and Boniface stated the transition was amicable with no hostile fork expected.

telegram · zaihuapd · Jul 21, 11:06

**Background**: Jellyfin is a free and open-source media server software suite that was forked from Emby in 2018. It allows users to stream their own media collection to any device, and has grown into a leading self-hosted media platform with no paid tiers or licensing restrictions. The project is maintained entirely by volunteers, and like many community-run projects, it faces challenges of maintainer burnout and governance.
